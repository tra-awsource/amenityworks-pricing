Cost floor recipe

  • Labor = hours × labor cost rate
  • Miles = round-trip miles × $/mile
  • Dump = landfill fees + mattress fees
  • Lead recovery = lead cost × multiplier (if you paid for the lead)
  • Truck = day rate × days allocated
  • Overhead = flat admin / insurance slice
  • Floor = sum of above — never quote below this
  • Healthy quote ≈ floor ÷ (1 − target margin%)

Four job types

One-off residential

  • Full retail rates
  • Min ~$95
  • Target margin ~45–55%
  • Home cleanouts, move-outs
  • Card / cash often same day

One-off commercial

  • ~10–15% above residential
  • Min ~$125
  • Target margin ~50–55%
  • Office purge, retail remodel, one-time site clean
  • COI, docks, Net 15/30, after-hours — price it

Recurring residential

  • ~20% off res retail
  • Min ~$75 if real volume
  • Target margin ~30–40%
  • HOA bulk days, estate multi-visit

Recurring commercial

  • Same recurring rate card
  • Contract: load cap + overage
  • Property managers, storage, offices
  • Invoice terms; still full dump/heavy adders

One-off commercial is not the same as one-off residential. Liability, certificates of insurance, loading docks, elevators, after-hours access, and slower pay all cost you — the commercial rate card covers that. Don’t give commercial customers residential pricing “to win the job.”

When to use which price

  • 1–3 known items → per-item (or min fee if sum is tiny)
  • Mixed pile / garage / estate → volume tiers
  • Hybrid → default: customer pays the higher of item total vs volume (protects you when pile is “a few” expensive items)
  • Brush / yard only → cubic yard rate + dump (separate from furniture tiers)

Sanity check on your current card

  • Volume ladder (⅛ $150 → full $600) is in a competitive independent range for Austin-area trailer work; nationals often run higher on full loads.
  • Per-item furniture/appliances look reasonable; sofa $95 and fridge $75–95 are usable if access is easy.
  • $25/hr is a cost rate, not a customer rate. Never sell the job as $25/hr — you’ll lose on dump and drive time.
  • 3× lead cost belongs in the floor: if a lead cost $40, recover $120 in the quote before profit.
  • $.35/mile is fine as cost; for long one-off trips consider a trip minimum or zone fee.
  • Landfill mattress fee ($20 default) auto-adds only when Mattress qty is set under Items — pass-through, don’t eat WM fees.
  • Paint disposal is $7.50/gallon at the dump — enter gallons under Adders; fee only appears when gallons > 0.
  • Hazmat / special handling is a flat fee for extra work (mess, packing, chemicals, HHW trip) — separate from paint $/gal.
  • Breakdown / demo / extraction covers furniture breakdown, light demo, and digging material out of dirt (pylons, buried wood, posts).
  • Full trailer $480 recurring vs $600 one-off is a clean ~20% loyalty discount — good structure.

Recurring contract tips

  • Define: pickup day, max load per visit, what’s excluded (hazmat, concrete, dirt).
  • Overage: if they exceed contracted ¼ load → charge next tier or per ⅛ overage.
  • No-show / cancel <12h: half minimum.
  • Review rates every 6–12 months (dump fees move).
