# AmenityWorks — Junk Hauling Pricing Guide

Austin, TX & surrounding areas  
Companion to **Junk Haul Pricer** (`index.html`)

---

## 1. Four job types (always pick one)

| | **One-off residential** | **One-off commercial** | **Recurring** (res or commercial) |
|--|-------------------------|------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Who | Home cleanout, move-out | Office purge, retail remodel, one-time business site | Weekly/biweekly, PM, HOA, storage |
| Rate card | Full retail | **~10–15% above** residential retail | ~15–25% off residential retail |
| Minimum | **$95** | **$125** | **$75** (only with real ongoing volume) |
| Target margin after true costs | **~45–55%** | **~50–55%** | **~30–40%** |
| Extras | Stairs, heavy, carry | Same + COI, docks, elevators, Net terms, after-hours | Contract load cap + overage |
| Pay | Often same day | Invoice / Net 15–30 common — price the wait | Monthly invoice |

**One-off commercial ≠ one-off residential.**  
Businesses need certificates of insurance, tighter windows, loading docks/elevators, more paperwork, and often slower pay. That premium is earned — don’t give them the house rate “to win the job.”

**Recurring is not “be cheap.”** It is **predictable revenue** for a **clear discount**. Write terms.

## 2. How customer price is set

### A. Per-item
Best for **1–3 known items** (couch, fridge, mattress).

### B. Volume (trailer fractions)
Best for **mixed piles**, garage cleanouts, “all this stuff.”

Your ladder (as entered in the app):

| Load | One-off res | One-off com (~15% up) | Recurring (~20% off res) |
|------|-------------|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Minimum 1–2 small | $95 | $125 | $75 |
| ⅛ trailer | $150 | $175 | $120 |
| ¼ trailer | $200 | $250 | $160 |
| ⅓ trailer | $250 | $300 | $200 |
| ½ trailer | $300 | $350 | $240 |
| ¾ trailer | $450 | $525 | $360 |
| Full trailer | $600 | $700 | $480 |

### C. Hybrid (recommended default)
Charge the **higher** of:

- sum of selected items, or  
- volume tier for the pile  

Protects you when “three items” is actually a sectional + sleeper + fridge.

## 3. True costs (not customer rates)

These are **what it costs you** so you don’t underbid:

| Input | Your starting point | Role |
|-------|---------------------|------|
| Labor | **$25/hour** | Cost of your time (or you + helper blended) |
| Miles | **$0.35/mile** | Fuel + wear (round trip) |
| Dump fee | Actual ticket | Always pass through / recover |
| Lead cost × **3** | If you paid for the lead | Recover marketing before profit |
| Trailer rental | **$125/day** (your cost) × days on that job | Full day if only one job; 0.5 if two jobs share one rental day |
| Overhead | Small flat per job | Phone, insurance, admin |

```
Floor = labor + miles + dump + (lead × 3) + truck share + overhead
Healthy quote ≈ Floor ÷ (1 − target margin%)
```

**Critical:**  
**$25/hr is not a customer hourly rate.**  
If you quote “$25/hr + dump,” a 1-hour sofa job at 20 miles loses money after dump and drive.

### Example floor (one-off ¼ trailer)

| | |
|--|--|
| Labor 1.5 h × $25 | $37.50 |
| 20 miles × $0.35 | $7 |
| Dump | $45 |
| Lead $0 | $0 |
| Trailer 0.5 day × $125 | $62.50 |
| Overhead | $15 |
| **Floor** | **~$167** |
| At 50% target margin | charge **~$334** |

Your card says ¼ one-off **$200** → **below cost-plus target** on that example. Either:

- raise ¼ toward **$250–$280**, or  
- cut truck allocation / hours / dump, or  
- accept thin margin only when the dump is cheap and drive is short  

Full trailer **$600** one-off is much healthier against a full-day cost stack.

## 4. Your rate card vs market (directional)

Industry junk removal is usually **volume tiers + per-item for singles**, not pure hourly.

- Nationals (e.g. full truck) often land **higher** than $600 in many metros.  
- Independent DFW/Austin-style cards often run roughly **min ~$95–150**, half **~$300–450**, full **~$500–800+**.  
- Your **$150 → $600** ladder is **usable and competitive** for a trailer-based independent.  
- Full load **$480 recurring / $600 res one-off / $700 com one-off** is a clean three-band structure.

Per-item examples you set (chair $40–50, sofa $95, fridge $75–95, mattress $75) are **reasonable for easy access**. Add stairs/heavy/long carry or you’ll underbid sleeper sofas and 2nd-floor fridges.

## 5. Adders (always)

| Adder | Your range | Note |
|-------|------------|------|
| Stairs / floor | $20–40 | App default $30/floor |
| Heavy (sleeper, safe, solid wood) | $50–100 | Default $75 |
| Breakdown / demo / **extraction** | $25–75 typical; **$75–150+** hard dig-outs | Furniture breakdown, light demo, **digging pylons/posts/wood from dirt** — count each task |
| Long carry >50 ft | $25 | Per long path |
| Mattress landfill fee | **$20 pass-through** (WM) | Only when mattress is in Items |
| Paint disposal | **$7.50 per gallon** pass-through | Only when gallons &gt; 0; don’t eat dump paint fees |
| Hazmat / special handling | **$15–$60 flat** (your fee) | Extra work: packing, mess, chemicals, HHW trip — not the dump’s $/gal |
| Tarp fee if required by yard | $30 | Avoid — tarp your loads |

**Paint vs hazmat:**  
- Dump paint fee = pass-through at $7.50/gal  
- Hazmat handling = *your* flat fee when the job is more work than a normal haul  

**Billing hazmat separately is normal — and an upcharge for handling is not wrong.**  
- Facility fee = what the dump charges (pass through)  
- Handling fee = your time/risk (you should charge this; it’s not “padding,” it’s the work)  
- Customer quote text states hazardous/special waste is **extra**, not “maybe refused.”
| Rush / after-hours | +15% each | In the app toggles |

**Brush:** $9.50/cu yd + fees — price separately from furniture volume (weight/density differs).

## 6. Recurring contract checklist

1. Frequency (weekly / biweekly / monthly)  
2. Included volume per visit (e.g. up to ¼ trailer)  
3. Overage price (next tier or $X per ⅛)  
4. What’s excluded (hazmat, dirt, concrete, tires)  
5. Access window and site contact  
6. Payment terms (Net 15 invoice vs card on file)  
7. Cancel / no-show fee  
8. Rate review every 6–12 months  

Commercial property work: prefer **per-item menus** or **fixed weekly flat** for budgeting; still measure overage by volume.

---

## 7. Which number to say on the driveway

1. Estimate load fraction (⅛ … full).  
2. Glance at heavy/stairs.  
3. Quote **volume + adders** (or hybrid if they listed items).  
4. Glance at app **true cost floor** — if quote &lt; floor, walk or raise.  
5. Recurring only after schedule + terms are real — don’t give recurring rates to a one-time tire-kicker.  
6. Businesses / offices / retail = **one-off commercial** unless a signed recurring contract exists.
